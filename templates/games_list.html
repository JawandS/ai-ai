About Experimental Economics

What is Experimental Economics?

Experimental economics uses controlled laboratory settings to study economic decision-making and social behavior. These games help researchers understand how people make choices involving cooperation, competition, and resource allocation.

  • • Study human behavior in economic contexts
  • • Test economic theories in controlled environments
  • • Understand cooperation and competition dynamics
  • • Explore decision-making under uncertainty

AI vs Human Participants

Our platform allows games with both AI agents and human participants, enabling unique research opportunities to compare artificial and human decision-making patterns.

  • • Compare AI and human strategies
  • • Study mixed groups of AI and humans
  • • Analyze learning and adaptation patterns
  • • Explore emergent group behaviors
